What is UXD?

User Experience Design (UXD) is the process of designing digital or physical products that are useful, easy to use, and delightful to interact with.

How to Get Started

  1. If you are new to the club and haven't officially signed up through BYU Clubs, be sure to do that! It's quick, easy, and FREE.
  2. Sign up for our for weekly newsletter for info about club events, job and internship opportunities, and links to tutorials and other learning content!
  3. Connect with us on these social media platforms. to receive by-the-minute updates about events and resources.
  4. Come to our club meetings every Thursday at 6 PM (check the event schedule for room locations).
  5. Get your club t-shirt for only $10 by filling out this form.

BYU's Design Thinking Minor

While there is no UXD major, BYU does offer a minor called "Design Thinking". It's a combination of design-related classes from several different programs, including:

  • the Design Program from the College of Fine Arts and Communications
  • the Experience Design and Management Program
  • the Technology & Engineering Studies Program from the College of Engineering
  • the Instructional Psychology and Technology Program
  • the Computer Science Department

The requirements can be met by completing between 12.5 and 16.5 credits.
